# Sensors and Actuators in Robotics

## Sensors
Sensors allow robots to perceive their environment.

### Common Types of Sensors:
- **Ultrasonic Sensors**: Use sound waves to measure distance to objects.
- **Infrared (IR) Sensors**: Detect light intensity or distance using IR light.
- **IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit)**: Combines accelerometer, gyroscope, and sometimes magnetometer to measure orientation and motion.
- **Lidar (Light Detection and Ranging)**: Uses laser pulses to create high-resolution 3D maps of environments.
- **Camera/Vision Sensors**: Provide visual data for computer vision tasks like object recognition and tracking.
- **Touch/Bumper Sensors**: Detect physical contact with objects.

### Applications:
- Obstacle avoidance
- Line following
- Mapping and localization (SLAM)
- Gesture recognition

## Actuators
Actuators are the components that allow robots to move and interact with the world.

### Common Types of Actuators:
- **DC Motors**: Provide continuous rotation, often used for wheels.
- **Servo Motors**: Allow precise control of angular position, ideal for robotic arms and steering.
- **Stepper Motors**: Move in discrete steps, providing high precision for positioning.
- **Pneumatic Actuators**: Use compressed air to create motion, useful for rapid or high-force tasks.
- **Hydraulic Actuators**: Use pressurized fluid, used in heavy-duty industrial robotics.

### Applications:
- Wheel locomotion
- Joint movement in robotic limbs
- Gripper actuation
- Fine-tuned positioning
