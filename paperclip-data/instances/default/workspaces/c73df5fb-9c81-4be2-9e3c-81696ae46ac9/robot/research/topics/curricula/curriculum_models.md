# Research: Educational Robotics Curricula Models

## 1. Competitive Robotics Models

**Overview**: These models use competition as a primary driver for engagement, teaching engineering design, teamwork, and problem-solving under pressure.

### Key Programs
- **FIRST Robotics (FIRST Tech Challenge, FIRST Robotics Competition)**:
    - **Focus**: Engineering design process, teamwork, and community involvement.
    - **Mechanism**: Teams build robots to complete specific tasks in structured games.
    - **Educational Value**: High emphasis on "Gracious Professionalism" and real-world engineering workflows.
- **VEX Robotics Competition**:
    - **Focus**: Mechanical engineering and structured programming.
    - **Mechanism**: Standardized kits and rulesets used globally in school leagues.
    - **Educational Value**: Provides a clear progression from middle school to high school/university levels.

## 2. Classroom-Integrated (Structured) Models

**Overview**: Designed for seamless integration into standard STEM curricula, providing teachers with ready-to-use lesson plans.

### Key Approaches
- **Standardized Kit Curricula (e.g., LEGO Education, VEX IQ)**:
    - **Focus**: Hands-on learning of physics, math, and logic concepts.
    - **Mechanism**: Highly scaffolded activities that align with grade-level learning objectives.
    - **Educational Value**: Low barrier to entry for teachers; high consistency in learning outcomes.
- **Embedded Systems Focus (e.g., Arduino Education)**:
    - **Focus**: Electronics, circuitry, and low-level programming.
    - **Mechanism**: Building custom electronic circuits and writing firmware.
    - **Educational Value**: Develops deep technical skills in electrical engineering and computer science.

## 3. Summary Table

| Model Type | Primary Driver | Complexity | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Competitive** | Competition & Teamwork | High | Middle/High School (Advanced) |
| **Classroom/Structured** | STEM Standards | Low/Medium | K-12 (General Education) |
| **Project-Based/Maker** | Individual Creativity | Medium/High | Hobbyists & Advanced Students |
