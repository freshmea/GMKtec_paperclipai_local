# Computer Vision in Robotics

Computer vision enables robots to interpret and understand visual information from their surroundings.

## Core Tasks
- **Object Detection**: Identifying and locating objects within an image or video stream (e.g., detecting a ball or a person).
- **Image Segmentation**: Partitioning an image into multiple segments to identify object boundaries (e.g., distinguishing the floor from a table).
- **Feature Extraction**: Identifying key points or patterns in an image (e.g., corners, edges, or textures) to aid in recognition and tracking.
- **Optical Flow**: Estimating the motion of objects between consecutive frames in a video sequence.
- **Depth Estimation**: Determining the distance of objects from the camera, often using stereo vision or structured light.

## Common Techniques and Models
- **Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)**: The backbone of most modern computer vision tasks, excellent at pattern recognition.
- **YOLO (You Only Look Once)**: A popular real-time object detection algorithm.
- **Transformer-based Vision Models**: Utilizing attention mechanisms for advanced visual understanding.
- **SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)**: Often relies on visual data (Visual SLAM) to navigate.

## Applications
- **Autonomous Navigation**: Avoiding obstacles and following paths.
- **Object Manipulation**: Identifying and grasping objects with robotic arms.
- **Human-Robot Interaction**: Recognizing gestures, facial expressions, or tracking human presence.
- **Quality Inspection**: Detecting defects in manufacturing processes.
