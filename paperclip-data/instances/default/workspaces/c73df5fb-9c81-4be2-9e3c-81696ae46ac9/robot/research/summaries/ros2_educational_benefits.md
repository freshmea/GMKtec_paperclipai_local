# ROS 2 in Robotics Education

## Overview
ROS 2 (Robot Operating System 2) is the industry-standard middleware for robotics, providing a modular and scalable framework. Its transition from ROS 1 makes it a critical subject for students learning modern, real-time, and production-ready robotics software.

## Key Advantages for Education

* **Real-Time Support:** Designed for real-time programming and embedded systems, bridging the gap between academic simulation and real-world hardware deployment.
* **Modern Architecture:** Leverages modern libraries and technologies, ensuring students learn current industry practices.
* **Scalability & Versatility:** Supports diverse computing environments, from single-board computers (SBCs) to heterogeneous computer clusters.
* **Modular & Reusable:** Offers a highly modular software framework that encourages the creation and reuse of specialized packages.

## Supported Ecosystem

### Hardware & Operating Systems
* **Operating Systems:** Primarily Linux (Ubuntu), with experimental support for macOS and Windows 10.
* **Hardware/SBCs:** Highly compatible with Raspberry Pi, BeagleBoard, Sitara ARM Processors, and various established robot platforms (e.g., TurtleBot, ROSbot).

### Educational Relevance
* **Research Foundation:** Serves as the primary platform for academic research at leading institutions like Stanford, MIT, and CMU.
* **Standardized Learning:** Provides a standardized suite of tools for teaching SLAM, navigation, manipulation, and perception.
* **Robust Ecosystem:** Offers extensive tools (e.g., `rviz`, `rosbag`), tutorials, and community support, making it the de facto choice for robotics curricula.
* **Practical Integration:** Facilitates hands-on learning through numerous ROS-compatible educational robot kits (e.g., GoPiGo3).
