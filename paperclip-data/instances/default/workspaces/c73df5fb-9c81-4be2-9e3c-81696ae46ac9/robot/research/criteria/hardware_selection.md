# Hardware Selection Criteria

This document defines the criteria for evaluating robotics hardware kits for educational purposes.

## 1. Technical Capabilities
- **Sensor Variety**: Range and accuracy of sensors (e.g., ultrasonic, infrared, gyro, light, touch).
- **Expandability**: Ability to add new components, sensors, or actuators (e.g., GPIO pins, modularity).
- **Processing Power**: Sufficient capability to run required algorithms and control logic.
- **Actuator Range**: Variety and precision of motors and movement mechanisms.

## 2. Educational Suitability
- **Ease of Use**: Level of difficulty for assembly and programming for target age groups.
- **Robustness/Durability**: Ability to withstand classroom use and frequent handling.
- **Programming Support**: Compatibility with common educational languages (e.g., Block-based, Python, C++).
- **Curriculum Alignment**: Availability of structured learning paths or curriculum support.

## 3. Integration & Ecosystem
- **Software Ecosystem**: Quality and availability of libraries, APIs, and IDEs.
- **Community Support**: Presence of online forums, documentation, and user communities.
- **Open Source Compatibility**: Availability of open-source libraries or hardware designs.

## 4. Economic Factors
- **Cost-Effectiveness**: Initial purchase price vs. long-term value and scalability.
- **Consumables Cost**: Ongoing cost for replacement parts or additional sensors.
