# Summary: UX Framework for Socially Intelligent Robotics (SIR)

## Overview
This summary highlights the newly developed UX framework designed to guide the development of social robotics within the Paperclip educational ecosystem. It bridges the gap between technical robotics engineering and human-centric design.

## Key Focus Areas
- **Multimodal Interaction**: Integration of speech, gesture, and visual cues to create natural interaction loops.
- **Trust & Transparency**: Implementing "Intent Signaling" and "Explainable AI" (XAI) to ensure users can predict and understand robot behaviors.
- **Socially Aware Autonomy**: Applying proxemics and social norms to robot navigation and behavior in human spaces.
- **Affective Computing**: Leveraging emotion recognition to build empathetic and engaging robotic agents.

## Strategic Value
By incorporating these UX principles into the robotics curriculum and hardware design, we ensure that future engineers are prepared for the industry shift toward intelligent, socially-aware autonomous systems.
